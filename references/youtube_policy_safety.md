# YouTube Policy, Copyright, AI, and YPP Risk Layer

Created by **Cruz Olli**

This reference provides risk-screening logic. It is not legal advice.

## High-risk patterns

- Reuploads with minimal changes.
- Compilation videos without substantial added value.
- Reaction videos with little original commentary.
- AI-generated bulk videos with no original viewpoint or human editorial layer.
- Subtitle-only, crop-only, speed-change-only, voiceover-only transformations.
- Unclear rights for music, footage, screenshots, or third-party clips.

## Safer patterns

- Original narration and argument.
- Creator's own experience and analysis.
- Critique, commentary, or education that clearly transforms the source.
- Original screen recordings, examples, demos, and visuals.
- Clear human editorial decisions.

## Practical rule

If the viewer cannot easily identify what the creator added, the video is operationally risky for monetization and brand trust.

## AI rule

AI can accelerate:

- Research organization.
- Script drafts.
- Title variants.
- Thumbnail concepts.
- Transcript cleanup.
- Edit review.

AI should not replace:

- Original judgment.
- Personal experience.
- Human credibility.
- Policy responsibility.
- Rights clearance.
