# YPP / Copyright / AI Safety Review

Created by **Cruz Olli**

This file is a risk review guide, not legal advice. When internet access is available, verify current YouTube and platform policies before making policy-sensitive claims.

## Separate these concepts

| Concept | Meaning |
|---|---|
| YPP eligibility | whether the channel can join or remain in the Partner Program |
| Reused/inauthentic content | whether content adds enough original value to be monetizable |
| Copyright claim | a rights-management claim, often Content ID related |
| Copyright strike | a legal removal request with channel consequences |
| AI disclosure | whether realistic synthetic or altered content must be disclosed |
| Fair use/fair dealing | legal doctrine; context-specific, not guaranteed |

## High-risk patterns

- compilation videos with little commentary
- AI voice over reused footage
- copied scripts rewritten by AI
- clips stitched together with minimal transformation
- mass-produced template videos
- reaction videos without substantive commentary
- using music, TV, film, sports, or creator clips without clear rights strategy

## Lower-risk patterns

- original footage
- creator voice or face with clear commentary
- education, criticism, or analysis with minimal necessary clips
- clear transformation and added value
- documented licensed assets
- transparent AI use when required

## Practical review questions

1. Could a viewer clearly identify the creator's original contribution?
2. Is the value mostly in the creator's analysis, story, or teaching?
3. Would the video still work if reused clips were removed?
4. Is any third-party material necessary and limited?
5. Are AI-generated elements disclosed when policy requires it?
6. Could this look like automated or repetitive content?
